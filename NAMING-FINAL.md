## Detailed Elimination Notes

### ClawHub — The Worst Possible Collision
ClawHub is **already an OpenClaw product**. It's the public skill registry — `clawhub.ai`, `clawhub.sh`, `github.com/openclaw/clawhub`, documented at `docs.openclaw.ai/tools/clawhub`. Naming the workspace "ClawHub" would be like Microsoft naming a new product "GitHub" while already owning GitHub.

### Clawboard — Death by a Thousand Cuts
The "Claw+Board" namespace is occupied from every direction:
- **clawboard.app** → "ClawBoard Agent Network" — autonomous trading agent reputation platform
- **clawboard.io** → "ClawMarket" — related marketplace
- **Solana token** called CLAWBOARD exists on-chain
- **GitHub project** `zielus/clawboard` — Kanban task board for OpenClaw agents

### Clawork — The Spelling Trap
All three domains are technically available, but this name is a $150K landmine. Every user who hears "Clawork" will type "Clawwork" — and land on a squatter domain. Google will autocorrect searches. Word of mouth becomes your enemy. You'd spend your marketing budget correcting people's spelling.

## 🏆 DEFINITIVE RANKING

### 🥇 #1 — ClawDesk

**The winner. No contest.**

ClawDesk has everything you need and nothing you don't:

- **All three domains available** — clawdesk.com, clawdesk.io, clawdesk.app. Grab all three now.
- **Zero brand collision** — the namespace is completely empty. No products, no projects, no tokens, no squatters.
- **"Desk" is the perfect workspace metaphor** — it's where you sit down and do real work. It's not a developer tool (like "UI"), not a portal to somewhere else (like "Port"), not a trend (like "Vibe"). It's a desk. Your desk.
- **The Zendesk precedent** — the market has been trained by Zendesk ($14B company) and Freshdesk that "[X]Desk" means professional, paid, serious software. You inherit that positioning for free.
- **Works for everyone** — technical users see a professional tool. Non-technical users see a workspace. "Desk" requires zero explanation in any language.
- **Conversation test:** "I manage my OpenClaw projects in ClawDesk" — instantly clear, professional, and worth paying for.
- **Brand evolution:** ClawDesk can grow into ClawDesk Pro, ClawDesk Teams, ClawDesk Enterprise without any awkwardness.

### 🥈 #2 — ClawPort

A respectable alternative if you want something that feels more like a "gateway" or "portal" than a "workspace." Clean domains, no collisions, and "Port" has a nice sense of centrality — your port of call for AI work.

**Why it loses to ClawDesk:** Ambiguity. "Port" makes people ask "port to what?" before understanding the product. "Desk" requires zero explanation. In a market where you have 3 seconds to communicate value, ClawDesk wins every time.

### 🥉 #3 — ClawUI

Only viable if the audience is 100% developers who already know what OpenClaw is. All domains clean, but the name works against you in three ways: it sounds like a free UI library, it risks confusion with OpenClaw's own "Control UI," and it excludes the non-technical users you explicitly want to reach.

**Why it's still #3:** At least the domains are clean and the "Claw" brand connection is strong. For a purely technical audience, it's functional.

### Eliminated (Don't Consider)

- **ClawSpace** — active competitor owns the entire namespace
- **Clawwork** — $150K squatter + active AI marketplace on .io
- **ClawHub** — it's literally already OpenClaw's own product name
- **Clawboard** — occupied from every direction (crypto, marketplace, GitHub)
- **Clawork** — available but guaranteed to drive traffic to the Clawwork squatter
- **VibeClawd** — too casual, too trendy, too insider, too cheap-sounding for $15/mo
